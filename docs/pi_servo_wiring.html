Pi 5 → PCA9685 → Servos · Wiring

Physical pin map for the connection between the Raspberry Pi 5 and the robotic hand. Four wires for logic, two for servo power, five for finger PWM.

1 · Pi 5 GPIO header — only 4 pins matter for this

Only pins 1, 3, 5, and 6 of the 40-pin header are used. Everything else stays free for future sensors / displays.

2 · Wiring map · Pi 5 ↔ PCA9685 ↔ Servos ↔ PSU

Pi 5 talks to the PCA9685 over I²C (4 wires). The PCA9685 fans out 5 PWM signals (yellow) to the finger servos. The 5 V PSU feeds the V+ rail through its own terminal block — and its ground joins the common-ground node along with the Pi's GND.

3 · Connection table · copy-paste reference

Wire From To Notes
red Pi 5 · header pin 1 (3.3V) PCA9685 · VCC chip logic supply only — not the servos
yellow Pi 5 · header pin 3 (GPIO 2 / SDA) PCA9685 · SDA I²C data, default address 0x40
orange Pi 5 · header pin 5 (GPIO 3 / SCL) PCA9685 · SCL I²C clock
black Pi 5 · header pin 6 (GND) PCA9685 · GND + PSU GND tie all three grounds at one point
red (heavy) 5 V PSU · positive PCA9685 · V+ screw terminal servo power — independent of Pi rail
black (heavy) 5 V PSU · negative PCA9685 · V+ GND screw terminal same screw block; must also tie to Pi GND
— Servo lead (thumb) PCA9685 · channel 0 (3-pin header) brown / black → GND row, red → V+ row, yellow → signal row
—Servo lead (index) PCA9685 · channel 1
—Servo lead (middle) PCA9685 · channel 2
—Servo lead (ring) PCA9685 · channel 3
—Servo lead (pinky) PCA9685 · channel 4
⚠ Common-ground rule: the PSU's negative terminal must connect to the Pi's GND (and the PCA9685's logic GND) at one point — typically right at the PCA9685's V+ GND screw. Without this, the PWM logic levels float relative to the servo power, and the servos either jitter or refuse to move.
