Bill of materials

×1

ESP32 DevKit V1

Wearable microcontroller: 12-bit ADC, dual-core, WiFi. Acquires both AD8232 channels at 1 kHz and UDP-streams to the Pi.

✓ have ~£8–12
×2

AD8232 Biopotential Module

Instrumentation amplifier + filter. One channel each. For ECG mode: chest leads (Lead I). For EMG mode: forearm flexors / extensors.

✓ have ~£8–24 each
×1

Raspberry Pi 5 (4 / 8 GB)

Receives UDP from ESP32, runs inference (PyTorch CNN), drives the PCA9685, serves the dashboard at :8000.

✓ have ~£60–80
×1

PCA9685 16-ch Servo Driver

I²C → 16 hardware PWM outputs at 50 Hz. Frees the Pi from precise timing, lets the servo PSU stay isolated from the Pi's 5 V rail.

○ need ~£8–12
×5

Micro Servo (MG90S or SG90)

One per finger. MG90S has metal gears and ~1.8 kg·cm — strong enough to pull tendon line. SG90 (plastic gears) is cheaper but wears faster.

○ need ~£3–6 each
×1

5 V / 6 V DC Supply (≥3 A)

Powers the servos ONLY (V+ on PCA9685). Must not share the Pi's 5 V rail — stall current would brown the Pi out.

○ need ~£10–15
